Part 6 · 01 Driving & Autonomous
The Drivetrain

Make the whole
robot move.

A tank drivetrain has just two controls: how fast the left wheels spin and how fast the right ones do. Everything a driver feels, straight, turning, spinning, comes from mixing those two numbers.

Two wheel groups, two numbers.

A differential (tank) drive splits the wheels into a left side and a right side. Spin both forward and you go straight; spin one faster and you curve; spin them opposite and you turn in place. Tank drive sets each side from its own stick. Arcade drive takes one stick (forward and turn) and mixes it into the same two wheel commands. Drive it.

Predict first
Before you run it: in arcade mode you give pure turn with zero forward (left = forward + turn, right = forward - turn). What do the two wheel sides do?
With forward at 0, the mix becomes left = +turn and right = -turn, so one side goes forward and the other backward and the robot spins in place. That is the whole trick of arcade: one stick splits into two opposite wheel commands. Run arcade, push pure turn, and watch the two wheel bars swing opposite ways.

🎯 Missions clear them with the sim above
○Pivot in place: in arcade, drag the stick straight sideways until Turn passes 0.50 while Forward stays under 0.10. Hold it for a second and watch the wheels run opposite.
○Carve a smooth arc: hold the stick so Left reads near 0.80 and Right near 0.40 for a second. That is forward with a little turn, like leaning a bike through a corner.
○Switch to tank and push only the L throttle past 0.50. With Right at zero the robot wheels around its stopped side, like paddling a canoe on one side.
★Gold: overflow the mix. In arcade, push the stick toward the top-right corner until Forward + Turn adds past 1.30. Watch Left pin at 1.00, that clipping is why real drive code scales both sides down together.
In code

WPILib does the mixing

You read the sticks, then hand them to DifferentialDrive. arcadeDrive mixes forward and turn for you; tankDrive takes the two sides straight:

DriveSubsystem Java · WPILib
DifferentialDrive drive = new DifferentialDrive(leftGroup, rightGroup);

// arcade: one stick, mixed into left/right under the hood
public void arcade(double fwd, double turn) {
  drive.arcadeDrive(fwd, turn);
}

// tank: each side gets its own command
public void tank(double left, double right) {
  drive.tankDrive(left, right);
}

// the mixing arcadeDrive does for you:
//   left  = fwd + turn
//   right = fwd - turn
Arcade and tank are the same drivetrain

The hardware never changes, only how you turn driver input into the two wheel commands. Most teams default to arcade because one stick for "go and steer" feels natural, but tank shines for precise pivots. Either way it bottoms out at two numbers: left wheel speed and right wheel speed.

Going further · optional

The plain arcade mixing can clip: command full forward plus full turn and left = 1 + 1 = 2, which a motor can not do, so it saturates at 1 and the robot turns less than you asked. WPILib's DifferentialDrive scales the two sides down together to preserve the turn, much like the desaturate step you will meet in swerve. Try logging the raw left and right values before clamping and watch them blow past 1. What would happen to the robot's path if you let one side clip to 1 while the other stayed in range?

Show the thinking

Clipping only one side changes the ratio between the two sides, and that ratio is what sets how sharply the robot turns. So the robot would turn less than you asked, because the side that wanted more than 1 gets held back while the other side keeps its value, shrinking the difference between them. Desaturating instead scales both sides down by the same factor, which keeps their ratio and preserves the turn, only at a lower overall speed.

Checkpoint

Quick check

Pick an answer to see why.

Question 1
On a tank drivetrain, how do you turn the robot in place?
Opposite wheel directions rotate the robot around its center with no forward motion. Equal-and-same spins it straight; unequal-and-same curves it.
Question 2
What does arcadeDrive do that tankDrive doesn't?
arcadeDrive takes forward and turn from a single stick and computes left = fwd + turn, right = fwd - turn. tankDrive skips the mixing and takes each side directly.
Question 3
In arcade mixing, a pure turn-right command (turn > 0, fwd = 0) produces…
With fwd = 0: left = 0 + turn (forward), right = 0 - turn (backward). The robot pivots in place toward the right. The mixing handles it automatically.
