Part 3 · 04 Power Transmission
Bearings

Hold the shaft,
let it spin.

Every gear, sprocket, and pulley rides on a shaft, and that shaft has to spin freely without flopping around. Bearings do both: low friction so it turns easily, and support so it runs true. Support it on one side only and it whips.

Two bearings beat one.

A shaft held by a single bearing is a diving board: load the end and it bends and wobbles as it spins. Support it with a bearing on each side of the load and it runs dead straight. Switch the support below and load it up.

The cantilever shaft bows under load and the end whirls in a circle as it spins, that wobble is runout, and it wrecks precision and wears parts. The supported shaft, pinned at both ends, barely moves no matter the load.

In the shop

Choosing and mounting bearings

FRC bearings are mostly small flanged ball bearings that press into a pocket. Two things matter: the bore must match the shaft, and the shaft should be supported on both sides of a load:

⚙️
Ball bearingLow-friction rings of balls; the standard way a shaft spins.
🕳️
Bore matches shaftA 1/2" hex-bore bearing for a 1/2" hex shaft; round bores for round shaft.
📍
Press-fit pocketThe bearing presses into a precise hole or a bearing block.
🌉
Support both sidesA bearing on each side of the load stops the wobble.
Cantilever only when you must

Supporting both ends means the second bearing needs its own plate or standoff, which is more parts and a tighter design. Sometimes that's worth avoiding, a short, lightly-loaded shaft can cantilever fine. But anything carrying real load, a drive wheel, a heavy gear, almost always wants two bearings so it doesn't deflect. When a mechanism feels sloppy or a gear is wearing on one side, an unsupported shaft is a prime suspect.

Checkpoint

Quick check

Pick an answer to see why.

Question 1
What does a bearing do for a shaft?
A bearing supports the shaft and lets it rotate freely with very little friction. Without one, the shaft would grind against its mount and bind.
Question 2
A loaded shaft is held by just one bearing. What happens as it spins?
A single bearing leaves the load end unsupported, like a diving board. It deflects under load and wobbles as it spins. Two bearings, one on each side, fix it.
Question 3
You're using 1/2" hex shaft. What bearing bore do you need?
The bearing's bore must match the shaft. A 1/2" hex shaft needs a 1/2" hex-bore bearing so the shaft fits and the hex can transmit torque through it.
