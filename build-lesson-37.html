Manufacturing Methods

The right
machine.

A workshop is a set of specialized tools, each good at one kind of cut. Knowing which machine makes which part, and which one is the safe, fast choice, is what turns a CAD model into real aluminum. Pick the right tool and the part takes minutes; pick wrong and you fight it for an hour.

Each job below has a best tool. Read the part, then pick the machine that's built for it, the goal is matching the cut to the tool that does it cleanly and safely.

Most FRC parts come off just a few machines, and the bandsaw, drill press, and a chop saw will make the vast majority of a robot. The fancy tools are for the few parts that truly need them.

🔄
LatheRound parts: shafts, spacers, anything turned about an axis.
Safety is the first manufacturing skill

Every one of these machines can hurt you in an instant: safety glasses always, no gloves or loose sleeves near spinning tools, hair tied back, and never walk away from a running machine. A part can be remade; a finger can't. The best shops are the ones where safety is automatic, and that discipline, like clean wiring, is a habit you build from day one. 3D printers and CNC routers extend what you can make, but the same rule holds: learn the tool, respect it, and ask before using one you haven't been trained on. The mill and lathe especially are not beginner machines: they grab and spin parts with no warning, so on most teams a trained mentor runs them or stands right beside you, and you never operate one you haven't been signed off on.

Checkpoint

Quick check

Pick an answer to see why.

Question 1
What's the workhorse for cutting tube and plate to length?
Saws cut stock to length, and they make most of the cuts on a robot. The lathe and mill are for special shapes.
Question 2
What's the lathe best at?
A lathe spins the part against a cutter, perfect for anything round and turned about an axis.
Question 3
What's the first manufacturing skill to learn?
Shop safety comes before any cut. Parts are replaceable; you are not. Safe habits are the foundation of every good shop.
