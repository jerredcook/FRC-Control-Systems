Question 1
A robot drives to a wall and stops at a fixed distance using a sensor, correcting itself the whole way. A second robot only runs its motors for 1.5 seconds and hopes. What is the key difference?
Closed loop means measure, compare to the goal, correct, repeat. The timed robot sets an action and never looks at the result, so it fails the moment the battery sags or the floor changes. Review: Lesson I.1 · Feedback & Control
Question 2
An elevator under pure proportional control with a gravity load settles a few centimeters below its target and stays there. Raising kP shrinks the gap but never closes it. Why?
This is droop, equal to g over kP. Output is kP times error, so the carriage parks exactly where the leftover error supplies the gravity-holding voltage. More kP shrinks it but cannot reach zero. Review: Lesson I.2 · Proportional Control
Question 3
That same elevator still parks below target. Which term is designed to drive that steady-state error to zero, and how?
The integral is the controller's memory: it adds up error every loop and keeps growing while any gap remains, eventually supplying the gravity-holding voltage so the proportional term can drive error to zero. Review: Lesson I.3 · Integral Control
Question 4
After adding integral, you raise kD to tame overshoot, but the motor starts buzzing and jittering on a noisy encoder. What is happening?
Derivative reacts to rate of change, so noise in the measurement gets magnified. Lower kD or filter the sensor. Buzzing on noise is the classic derivative symptom; windup instead shows up as overshoot after long moves. Review: Lesson I.4 · Derivative Control
Question 5
Instead of fighting droop with feedback, you measure the mechanism with a SysId routine and add feedforward. Which gain holds an elevator against gravity, and what does feedforward let you do?
kG is the constant gravity-holding voltage for an elevator, measured by SysId along with kS, kV, kA. Feedforward predicts the bulk of the voltage from physics, so the PID feedback only corrects the small remaining error. It does not replace feedback, it only shrinks its job. Review: Lesson I.6 · Feedforward
