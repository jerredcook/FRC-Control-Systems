Question 1
A team moves their elevator's closed loop off the roboRIO and onto the Kraken's built-in TalonFX. The main reason competitive teams do this is that the TalonFX runs the loop at:
The TalonFX has its own processor and closes the loop at roughly 1000 Hz, versus the roboRIO's 50 Hz. Less delay means higher gains stay stable and disturbances get corrected far sooner.
Question 2
You store kS, kG, kV, kA and kP, kI, kD in Slot0 of a TalonFXConfiguration, then send a request. For an arm, which GravityType tells the on-motor loop to handle gravity correctly, and why?
An arm's gravity load follows cosine of angle, so Arm_Cosine scales kG by cos(angle) on the device at 1 kHz. Elevator_Static applies a constant kG and is correct for an elevator, where gravity does not change with position.
Question 3
To protect the circuit breaker and the motor windings, you set current limits in the TalonFXConfiguration. You also want the elevator to hold position when disabled rather than drift down. Which two settings address these?
Stator and supply current limits cap torque and protect the breaker. NeutralMode Brake resists motion when the motor is idle so the carriage holds. Motion Magic settings shape moves; SensorToMechanismRatio and FusedCANcoder handle sensing, not holding or protection.
Question 4
You send a raw PositionVoltage request and the elevator lunges hard off the line. You switch to Motion Magic instead. What does Motion Magic do differently from a single request?
Motion Magic builds an onboard motion profile bounded by a cruise velocity and acceleration, so the move eases in instead of lunging. It still uses the Slot0 feedforward and feedback gains to track that profile at 1 kHz.
Question 5
Rather than guessing gains, you run a Phoenix 6 SysId routine and fit a line to the voltage-versus-velocity data. The slope and intercept give you which gains, connecting back to Part I's system identification?
The quasistatic ramp plots voltage against velocity. The slope is kV, the cost of speed, and the zero-velocity intercept bundles kS (friction) and kG (gravity), exactly the SysId result from Part I. The dynamic step test then reveals kA.
