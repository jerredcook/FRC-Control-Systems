The Control System

The robot's
nervous system.

The roboRIO is the brain, the radio is its link to the driver station, and the CAN bus is the nerve that carries commands to every motor controller and sensor. The catch with the CAN bus: it's one long daisy chain, and a break anywhere takes out everything past it.

Everything chains off the roboRIO.

Your code runs on the roboRIO. Its commands travel down the CAN bus, a single two-wire loop that hops from one device to the next in a daisy chain. Each device taps the same two wires. Click a link below to cut the chain and watch what goes dark.

A loose CAN wire is the classic mystery failure

Because the CAN bus is a chain, a single bad crimp or unplugged connector silences every device after it, and the symptom (half the motors suddenly unresponsive) looks baffling until you trace the chain. This is why wiring craftsmanship matters so much: secure connectors, good crimps, and strain relief. The robot's data, like its power, is only as reliable as the weakest connection. Checkpoint

Quick check

Pick an answer to see why.

Question 1
What is the roboRIO?
The roboRIO is the brain. Your program runs on it, reads sensors, and sends commands out over the CAN bus to the motor controllers.
Question 2
How are the motor controllers connected to the roboRIO?
CAN is a single two-wire bus that hops device to device in a daisy chain. Each controller taps the same two wires, no separate cable per device.
Question 3
A connector midway down the CAN bus comes loose. What happens?
A break in a daisy chain cuts off everything downstream of it. That's why a single loose CAN connector can knock out half the robot, and why crimps and strain relief matter.
