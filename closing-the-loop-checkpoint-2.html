Question 1
In a WPILib robot, where does the control loop you wrote in Part I actually run, and how often?
The roboRIO runs a fixed 20 ms (50 Hz) loop. The CommandScheduler calls each subsystem's periodic() every beat, so read sensor, compute output, apply voltage happens there about 50 times a second.
Question 2
Your elevator uses a motor geared 10:1 driving a drum 0.05 m in diameter. The encoder reports motor rotations, but your code treats them as meters. The carriage flies past every target. What is the fix?
Unit and gear-ratio mistakes are the number one real-robot bug. Motor rotations divided by 10, times pi times 0.05 m, gives the carriage height in meters. No gain tuning fixes a units error.
Question 3
You wire up WPILib's PIDController for the elevator and call it correctly, but with pure P it still parks a little below target, exactly like the Part I simulation. How do you call it, and why the droop?
PIDController.calculate takes the measurement first, then the setpoint. The droop is the same behavior from Lesson 2 showing up in code: pure P leaves a steady-state error against a gravity load.
Question 4
You add feedforward in code. For an arm, the WPILib ArmFeedforward needs the arm angle, but for an elevator the ElevatorFeedforward does not. Why does the arm need the angle?
An arm held horizontal fights full weight (cos 0 = 1); pointed straight up it barely strains (cos 90 = 0). ArmFeedforward scales kG by cos(angle). An elevator's gravity load is constant, so kG alone holds it anywhere.
Question 5
You want smooth, fast moves that never slam the mechanism. You wrap everything in a ProfiledPIDController and feed the profile's current velocity to feedforward. What does the motion profile buy you, and why feed its velocity to feedforward?
A TrapezoidProfile plans a ramp-up, cruise, ramp-down move within velocity and acceleration limits. Feeding the profile's instantaneous velocity to ElevatorFeedforward means kV and kA predict the voltage for the planned motion, not a static hold, keeping feedback's job small the whole way.
