Going further · optional

Passing inspection is far easier when the hardest checks are tracked all season, not discovered at the table. Weight is the classic one: experienced teams keep a running weight budget from the first week, since CAD can total a part's mass before it exists, and they watch it climb so nothing has to be hacked off the night before. Try this thought experiment: if your robot came in two pounds over, what is the first thing you would lighten, and how much would that one change actually save? Knowing the answer before you build is the difference between a calm inspection and a frantic one.

Show the thinking

There is no single right part, but the smart targets are heavy material you can remove without hurting strength: lightening pockets and holes in structural plate, swapping a solid part for tube, or trimming overbuilt brackets. The point of the exercise is that you should already know the candidates and roughly what each saves, because your CAD has a running mass for every part. Pull two pounds out of a few plates through pockets and the inspection stays calm; discover it the night before and you are hacking on the real robot. Avoid lightening anything that carries a real load, like a drive gearbox mount, since that trades weight for a failure on the field.
