The five-question triage

Ask these, in order

When anything misbehaves, walk down this list
  1. Does it move the right way? If not - motor or sensor inverted.
  2. Are the numbers sane? Reads 2 m at 0.2 m - wrong gear ratio / units.
  3. Does it hold position? Sags below target - missing kG (feedforward).
  4. Is it stable? Oscillates - too much kP, too little kD.
  5. Is it safe? Hot motor, tripped breaker - no current limit.

Quick check

Pick an answer to see why.

Question 1
On the scope, the position is a steady sine wave around the setpoint. The most likely cause?
Oscillation is the signature of too much proportional gain: every correction overshoots, so it swings back and forth. Lower kP, or add kD to damp it.
Question 2
The elevator settles a few centimeters below every target and just stays there. The fix?
A constant droop is gravity winning. Pure P needs leftover error to make holding voltage; feedforward kG supplies it outright, so the error goes to zero. (Integral also works, but slower.)
Question 3
A swerve module's wheel points the wrong way the instant the robot powers on. Most likely cause?
The internal rotor only counts from wherever it booted, so steering starts from a wrong zero. A FusedCANcoder gives the true absolute angle immediately - the same fix as the arm.
