The Mechanism Toolbox

A few shapes,
endless robots.

Almost every FRC robot is built from the same small set of mechanism archetypes. Once you can name them, designing a robot becomes a matter of choosing the right ones for the game and bolting them together.

Match the job to the mechanism.

Reach high, grab low, launch far, place gently, each is a job a particular archetype is built for. Read the task and pick the mechanism you'd reach for.

The archetypes

This is the whole toolbox. Every mechanism in the rest of this part is one of these, and the rest of building a robot is choosing and combining them:

Elevator / liftMoves something straight up and down. Great for vertical reach.
💪
ArmA rotating joint that swings a tool through an arc.
🔗

🌀

🎯

🤏

Pick the simplest thing that wins

The temptation is to build the fanciest mechanism you can. The better instinct is to ask what the game actually rewards and choose the simplest archetype that does it reliably. A robot that does one job flawlessly beats one that does three jobs poorly. When you're stuck, look at what the best teams build, it's almost always a clean combination of these few shapes, not something exotic.

Going further · optional

Pick a real game you have watched and try to name the mechanisms on a robot you liked, then count them. You will usually find two or three working archetypes, not six. A useful design game: give yourself a strict budget of, say, two mechanisms and decide which jobs are worth your motors and which you skip. Learning what to leave off is harder, and often more valuable, than learning what to add.

Show the thinking

Spend your two mechanisms on the scoring actions that earn the most points and that you can do reliably every match, then let the drivetrain itself be a mechanism by playing defense or moving game pieces. The jobs worth cutting are the ones that add a whole subsystem for a few extra points, depend on a flawless partner, or only pay off in the last seconds of a match. A robot that does two things every single match almost always beats one that attempts five and breaks, because in eliminations a mechanism that works 80 percent of the time fails roughly one match in two of a best-of-three. Good looks like a short, honest list where every item earns its weight and you can name what you chose not to build.

Quick check

Pick an answer to see why.

Question 1
What is a mechanism archetype?
Archetypes are the standard mechanism shapes, elevators, arms, four-bars, intakes, shooters, grippers, that nearly every robot is built from. You pick and combine them for the game.
Question 2
You need to launch a game piece across the field. Which archetype?
Launching a piece at distance is exactly what a flywheel shooter does: spin a wheel up and feed the piece into it. An elevator lifts; a gripper holds.
Question 3
When in doubt about which mechanism to build, what's the best instinct?
Simplicity wins. A robot that does one job flawlessly beats one that does several poorly. Choose the simplest archetype that scores reliably.
