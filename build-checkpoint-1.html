Question 1
A teammate walks up to the bandsaw wearing thin work gloves to handle the sharp aluminum. They are about to start cutting. What should you say?
On any spinning or moving cutter, a glove that snags drags your hand into the machine. Bare hands pull away cleanly. Save gloves for handling sharp stock by hand, never for running the tool.
Question 2
You need to drill four clean holes in a small steel plate. The plate keeps shifting under the bit. The right move is to:
Always clamp; an unclamped part can grab the bit and spin like a propeller. Steel also wants a slower speed than wood or aluminum, since too much speed burns the bit dull and work-hardens the metal.
Question 3
A drawing calls for a hole spaced 2.000 ± 0.005 in from the edge. Which measured value passes, and what tool reads it?
The tolerance allows 1.995 to 2.005 in, so 1.997 passes and 2.040 is way out. A caliper reads to 0.001 in, the precision parts need to line up; a tape or ruler is too coarse for this.
Question 4
A 1/4-20 bolt on a vibrating drivetrain keeps backing out mid-match. What fixes it?
A nylock nut grips the threads with a nylon ring, and blue threadlocker bonds them. Over-tightening only strips the threads, and a metric bolt will not match a 1/4-20 thread, so it would not hold at all.
Question 5
You strip the hex socket on a screw because the Allen key was a hair too small and spun inside the head. What is the lesson for next time?
A driver one size off cannot grip and rounds the head into a stripped, hard-to-remove fastener. Socket-head cap screws take a hex key, so match the size and seat it fully before you turn.
